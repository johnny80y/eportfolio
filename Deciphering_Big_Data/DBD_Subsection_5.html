Compliance (Unit 8)

Deciphering Big Data


Second Peer Discussion – Initial Post

(05 March 2023)

The German Federal Office for Information Security (BSI) developed its own compendium of IT security standards called the “IT-Grundschutz” (BSI, 2017). It outlines general standards for information security management systems (ISMS) and is based on ISO27001. Thus, both certifications are closely aligned (BSI, 2021).

The EU’s (2016) General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) sets out to achieve a different goal. While the IT-Grundschutz and ISO27001 focus on ISMSs and organizational security, the GDPR focuses on individual privacy and accountability (Diamantopoulou et al., 2020). Nonetheless, all aspects of data protection are at least partially addressed with a well-developed ISMS (Hanschke, 2018). In particular, both standards require organizations to implement accountability and clear responsibilities within the organization, regular risk assessments, and continual testing of their security frameworks (Lopes et al., 2019).

Some of the topics covered by the GDPR that are not addressed in ISO27001 are consent, data portability, and individual’s rights to be forgotten or to object to certain kinds of data processing (Lopes et al., 2019). As the GDPR lacks technical implementation recommendations, ISO27001 can be a great starting place for organizations.

In summary, the GDPR and ISO27001 have many similar goals, and implementing the ISO standards will move organizations a long way in the direction of GDPR compliance. However, protecting individuals’ rights and the requirement of consent go beyond the scope of ISO27001. Thus, organizations need to carefully incorporate consent and rights into the planning of their ISMS. In doing so, possible conflicts between the GDPR and ISO27001 must be carefully weighed. Privacy and security go hand-in-hand but can also come into conflict. For example, scanning emails for security purposes sometimes requires human analysts to look at potential threats. Processes must be in place that protects customers’ and employees’ privacy in such cases.
